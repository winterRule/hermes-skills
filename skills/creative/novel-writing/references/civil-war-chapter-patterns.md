# Civil War Chapter Patterns (内战章节模式)

**Validated across Arc 17 (421-455)**: Civil war chapters are systematically shorter than battle chapters when drafted with the same vignette count.

## Why Civil War Chapters Run Short

| Factor | Battle Arc (外战) | Civil War Arc (内战) |
|:--|:--|:--|
| Dramatic tension | Built-in (life vs death) | Moral complexity (brother vs brother) |
| Physical action | Combat sequences fill CJK | Restraint, waiting, refusal — less action |
| Antagonist | Clear (瀛烬) | Unclear (former comrades) |
| Emotional core | Survival | Guilt, loyalty, identity |

## Object Confrontation Technique (物件对峙)

Civil war tension lives in objects, not firefights. Every chapter needs an object that embodies the split:

- 江寒's 铜质工牌 (past identity) vs 梭镖 (present duty)
- 铁柱's 齿盘 (being taken,凿号 remaining)
- 老陈's 灯 (still lit, regardless of who's fighting)
- 秦老's 搪瓷碗 (碗底「不止」— neither side can claim it)

**Rule**: Each civil war chapter must have at least one object that passes between or confronts both sides.

## Vignette Budget

| Chapter type | Battle arc vignettes | Civil war vignettes |
|:--|:--|:--|
| Action-heavy | 3-4 → 2000+ CJK | 5-6 → 2000+ CJK |
| Quiet/reflective | 4-5 → needs expansion | 5-6 → borderline, budget 1-2 expansion rounds |

**Arc 17 empirical data (full, 40 chapters)**: Chapters 421-430 (political negotiation, 8/10 expanded), 431-435 (breakdown, 4/5 expanded), 436-440 (building, 4/5 expanded), 441-445 (conflict/soft prison, 5/5 expanded), 446-450 (final push, 5/5 expanded), 451-460 (civil war opening, 10/10 expanded). Across 40 chapters, only 2-3 passed on first draft. The civil war sub-arc (451-460) was the WORST: first drafts 867-1453 CJK, every single chapter needed 2-3 expansion rounds. The political negotiation sub-arc (421-430) was the best: first drafts 1738-3075 CJK (richer in dialogue and political maneuvering, which naturally fills CJK).

## Pre-Writing Checklist for Civil War Chapters

1. Identify the object that carries the chapter's moral tension
2. Plan 5 distinct vignettes (character + object + sensory detail)
3. Each vignette must show both sides of the split (not just the protagonist's side)
4. Include at least one scene where a character from the "other side" appears as a human being
5. Close with an object in continuity (灯还在, 罐还在, 水还在流)
