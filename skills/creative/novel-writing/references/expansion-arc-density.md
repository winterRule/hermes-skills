# Expansion Arc Density Problem

When inserting new chapters between existing ones in expansion arcs, first drafts are systematically too short because each chapter covers less plot distance.

Data: Arc 4 (076-090, 15 chapters) took 4 expansion rounds and 60+ tool calls. First drafts averaged 700-1600 CJK vs 2000+ target.

Root cause: In original arcs, 10 chapters spanned a full narrative arc. In expansion arcs, the same arc is spread across 20-25 chapters with each chapter filling a smaller narrative gap. The default 3-5 vignettes/chapter produces only 700-1600 CJK.

Solution: Plan 5-6 vignettes per chapter for expansion arcs. Add at least one atmospheric vignette (pure scene-setting) and one interiority vignette (character memory/fear/decision) that deepen character and world without requiring plot advancement.

Alternate technique: Use flashback/parallel-cut pattern, inserting 1-2 paragraphs cutting to another character's perspective or a past event echoing the current scene. Adds 200-400 CJK per insertion without disrupting narrative flow.
